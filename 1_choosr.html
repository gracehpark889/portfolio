Choosr
We make the decisions for you.

Overview

Choosr is a mobile app that serves as a way for users to quickly make decisions when out in large groups. The purpose of this case study is to alleviate the painpoint of decision making to help parties carry on with the day by randomizing location suggestions for user and friends.

ROLE:
User Experience & Interface Design, User Research

TEAM:
Nicholas Gagnon

TOOLS USED:
Adobe XD, Sketch, Invision, Photoshop

DURATION:
2 weeks

Research
In order to gain a deeper understanding of the problem at hand, five user interviews were planned and conducted to gain insight regarding pain points and future opportunities. Participants were asked to share about their experience when collectively making decisions of where to go when out with friends. Survey data of 30 participants were also collected in order to gain deeper user insight.

  • Key Takeaways

    • One strong suggestion helps individuals come to a conclusive decision.
    • Distance, price, and accommodation are taken into consideration when deciding where to go.
    • Individuals, more often than not, take into consideration the opinion of others before themselves in group settings.

User Persona
After we analyzed our data, a general user persona was created in order to grasp our target audience. Sean Reynolds is a 23 year old UX/UI designer that doesn’t like to idly spend his time. Sean’s pain points and needs were used as the foundation of our design thinking and creation.
Competitor Analysis
In order to differentiate Choosr from the competition, a competitor analysis was done to compare and contrast similar apps on the market. By analyzing the strengths and weaknesses of each application, a better understanding of how Choosr would be built was thought out throughly.
User Flow
To cater to the demographic of users who would be interested in using Choosr, simplicity was our first priority. We took away the classic on-boarding process by allowing users to go straight to the main purpose of the app. Our user flow was built to ensure intuitive actions and straight forward accessibility.
Feature Prioritization
Specific features that would have high impact to our application were highlighted and added in order to create a more fluid user flow for the user. Features such as map and Yelp integration as well as fluid animations were a must-have for our finalized prototype.
Sketching & Wireframing
Early iteration of sketches began in order to gain a general understanding of the layout of the mobile application following our user flow.
Testing
We put our designs to the test by observing how easily participants were able to navigate through Choosr with three given scenarios in our low fidelity prototypes.

Visual Design
With the data collected, designing Choosr's prototype to be presentable was our next priority. Playful colors and modern text were incorporated to allow the process of using the app simple and fun. We inferred that users would most likely be viewing Choosr with others which is why we made sure the buttons took up the full width of the screen as well as enlarging text.
