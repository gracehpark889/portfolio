Choosr
Choosr is a mobile app that serves as a way for users to quickly make decisions when out in large groups.


My Role: User Experience & Interface Design, User Research
Tools Used: Adobe XD, Sketch, Invision, Photoshop
Timeframe: 2 weeks


The Problem
Individuals are indecisive when coming to a conclusive decision when spending time out with peers. Various amounts of time is allocated in deciding what to do next to carry on with the day. We hypothesized that users are instinctively indecisive which leads to prolonged decision making.


The Solution
Allow the decision making process to be simplified by creating a more interactive and straightforward application. Choosr assists in speeding up the process by randomizing suggestions for user and friends to make quick decisions.

Research
In order to gain a deeper understanding of the problem at hand, five user interviews were planned and conducted to gain insight regarding pain points and future opportunities. Participants were asked to share about their experience when out with friends to hone in on the hypothesized problem of indecisiveness. Surveys were also sent out in order to gain further information to gain deeper user insight.

Based on the survey results of 30 participants, 59.2% of participants leaned more towards being decisive. Different from our initial hypothesis, the results made us wonder, if participants lean more to being decisive, why do users seem to generally become indecisive in group settings?

  • Key Takeaways
    • One strong decision helps individuals come to a conclusive decision.
    • Distance, price, and accommodation are taken into consideration when deciding where to go.
    • Our survey data differed from our hypothesized problem.
Competitor Analysis
In order to differentiate Choosr from the competitions, a competitor analysis was done in order to gain some major takeaways on how improvements could be made. Various application had similar goals and through the research, a better understanding of how Choosr would be built was thought out thoroughly.
User Persona
Based on the user interviews and surveys conducted, a general user persona was created in order to grasp our target audience. Sean Reynolds is a 23 year old UX/UI designer that doesn’t like to idly spend his time. Sean’s paint points and needs were used as the foundation of our design thinking and creation.
User Flow
To cater to the demographic of users who would be interested in using Choosr, simplicity was our first priority. We took away the classic on-boarding process by allowing users to go straight to the main purpose of the app. Our user flow was built to ensure intuitive actions and straight forward accessibility.
Feature Prioritization
Specific features that would have high impact to our application were highlighted and added in order to create a more fluid user flow for the user. Features such as map and Yelp integration as well as fluid animations were a must-have for our finalized prototype.
